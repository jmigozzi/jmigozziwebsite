Julien Migozzi


My name is Julien Migozzi and I am a Research Associate in the School of the Geography and the Environment at the University of Oxford, as a member of the ERC project Cities in Global Financial Networks.

As an urban and economic geographer, my research interests lie at the intersection of financial geography, urban studies and economic sociology.

My work explores on how real estate technologies and finance reconfigure housing markets and renew forms and processes of urban segregation, focusing on post-apartheid South Africa.

I use mixed methods that combines qualitative approaches (interview, participant-observation) with computational and spatial analysis, relying on open source tools such as R or QGIS.

Prior to joining the University of Oxford, I held appointments at the Ecole Normale Supérieure in Paris. I earned my MA degree in Geography from the Ecole Normale Supérieure de Lyon and my PhD from the University of Grenoble Alpes. I’m a Research Associate at the Research Labs Géographie-cités and PACTE.

I also occasionally write - and tweet - about rugby, especially when magic happens.
